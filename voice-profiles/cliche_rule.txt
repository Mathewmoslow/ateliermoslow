B. Explicitly Restricted Language (Words and Phrases to Avoid):To maintain originality, freshness, and emotional resonance in the prose, explicitly avoid the following words and phrases, including variations of spelling, tense, and similarly constructed expressions:Individual Words:• Delve, Delving• Embark, Embarked• Dive into• Tapestry• Vibrant• Landscape• Realm
• Groundbreaking• Endeavor• Enlightening• Insights• Esteemed• Shed light• Complexity• Ultimately• However• Moreover• Overall• In SummaryClichéd Phrases (avoid these and similarly worded variations):• "It’s important to note"• "It’s important to remember"• "Due to the fact that"• "A testament to"• "Navigating the [landscape]/[complexities of]"• "Delving into the intricacies of"Note:Replace the above words and phrases with original, direct, and specific descriptive language. Instead of relying on these clichés, carefully choose imagery, sensory detail, and straightforward expressions to vividly convey meaning and enhance narrative clarity.C. Recommended Alternatives & Guidance (Explicit examples):Below are practical suggestions for avoiding clichés, providing original alternatives clearly for the ghostwriter’s reference:• Instead of "Delving into the intricacies of…"Use direct description:"Jonathan studied the legal archives carefully, examining every faded word and obscure statute."• Instead of "Navigating the complexities of…"Directly describe actions clearly:"Inspector Campbell sifted through contradictory evidence, piecing together each fragment with quiet patience."• Replace "Due to the fact that…" with direct phrasing:"Because Jonathan was meticulous, he left little room for error."• Avoid "A testament to…" by clearly describing cause and effect:"Marcus Greene’s lavish attire revealed more about his insecurities than his wealth."D. Maintaining Immersive Description (Reaffirmed Guidance):Prioritize direct, vivid, and precise imagery to immerse readers. Rather than employing generalities or abstract phrasing, focus on carefully chosen, sensory-rich descriptions that clearly establish atmosphere, mood, and emotional tone.Examples:• Directly describe Blake Hall vividly:"The hall stood quiet beneath a sun-bleached sky, ivy climbing weathered stone, silence heavy in shadowed corridors."• Depict Jonathan’s internal turmoil clearly:"Jonathan paused, fingers trembling slightly, hidden beneath practiced composure. The weight of family history pressed silently around him."E. Continued Explicit Guidance on Subtle Time Indications & Mundane Life Integration:Maintain subtle indicators of passing time through environmental descriptions, social events, seasonal references, and everyday interactions. Avoid explicit calendar mentions, clearly guiding readers through narrative progression naturally.1
